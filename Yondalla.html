(yahn-DAH-lah)

Yondalla has two aspects: the Provider and the Protector. As the Provider, she is a goddess of fertility and growing things, of birth and youth. She can make barren things fertile and increase the growing rate of plants and animals to any speed she chooses. What she can give, she can also take away; with a wave of her hand she can age and wither creatures.

As the Protector, Yondalla wards off evil influences and intrusions (thus her shield symbol) and gives hobbits the strength and determination to defend themselves. In this aspect, Yondalla most often uses her illusionist powers to protect her worshipers. Hobbits set aside one day per week for worship of Yondalla (called "Safeday"), a day that is most spent in rest and play.

Clerics of Yondalla are concerned with all spheres of hobbit life, save thievery. They are protectors, watchers over home and fields, and community leaders. They officiate at weddings and funerals. Some use proficiencies and spells to protect agriculture, together with Sheela Peryroyl's druids. More light-heartedly, they organize the great feasts of the hobbit year, and many are superb cooks! Clerical level titles are as follows: Blessed Sister/Brother, Sacred Guardian, Revered Nurturer, Blessed Mother/Father, Eminent Prodigal, August Warden, Hallowed Provider, Exalted Protector, and Revered Councilor.
