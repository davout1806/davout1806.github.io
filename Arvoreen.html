(ahr-voh-REEN)

The closest thing the hobbits have to a god of war is Arvoreen the Defender, the patron of hobbit fighters. Arvoreen never attacks an opponent first, but the first attack upon the deity is either reduced in damage or reflected back upon the caster. For his part, Arvoreen can become invisible at will and attack unseen against any mortal, non-divine being. He uses a sword that can shape change into any other type of hand-to-hand weapon he desires. Arvoreen may summon hobbits heroes armed with short swords and short bows; they will obey him implicitly.

Arvoreen, though quite powerful, is not a particularly aggressive deity. He will only engage in combat if he is attacked, though he does seek his enemies and actively confronts them to get them to desist from their evil practices. He will not go very far out of his way to avoid combat if it occurs, however, and will fight to the finish.

Fighters and fighter/thieves comprise the majority of his worshipers; the fighter/thieves are sometimes of neutral good alignment, and all of them will prefer to use their fighting skills to their thieving ones. While Arvoreen does not hold thieving to be necessarily dishonorable, he discourages the use of such skills unless employed against enemies to better the chances for physical combat later. (It would be permissible, for example, to steal their weapons, or break into their camp to free prisoners, or make guerilla raids, but it is not good to steal just to make oneself rich.) Arvoreen absolutely forbids stealing from other hobbits or allied beings.

Arvoreen's clerics may be multi-classed cleric/fighters. They are protectors and defenders, constructing defense barriers, signaling systems, beacons, and traps. They procure weapons for hobbit communities and adventure to gain magical weapons and defensive items of all kinds. Clerical level titles are as follows: Shield Bearer, Warder, Guardian, Defender, Protector, Magistrate, Sheriff, Marshal, and High Marshal
