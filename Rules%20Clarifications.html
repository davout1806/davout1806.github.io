Damage and Exhaustion

Exhaustion can halve Hit point maximum. How does this interact with existing damage?

Two scenarios

One

Say your character has a Hit Points max of 30. Character gets hit and takes 10 points of damage. Then receives another level of exhaustion that causes "Hit point maximum halved". How much damage can the character receive before falling unconscious?

  1. Starting Hit Point Maximum: 30 HP
    • Damage Taken: 10 points
    • Current HP after damage: 30 - 10 = 20 HP
  2. New Exhaustion Level
    • Hit point maximum is halved.
    • New maximum HP: 30 ÷ 2 = 15 HP
  3. Current Situation:
    • Maximum HP: 15
    • Current HP: 15 (reduced from 20 due to the new maximum)
  4. Remaining HP before unconsciousness:
    • The character will fall unconscious when they reach 0 HP.
    • Therefore, they can take 15 more points of damage before falling unconscious.

So, the character can receive 15 more points of damage before falling unconscious. The key point is that when the hit point maximum is reduced due to exhaustion, the current hit points are also reduced to match if they exceed the new maximum. The previous damage doesn't further reduce the HP beyond this new maximum.

Two

Say your character has a Hit Points max of 30. Character gets hit and takes 25 points of damage. Then receives another level of exhaustion that causes "Hit point maximum halved". How much damage can the character receive before falling unconscious?

  1. Starting Hit Point Maximum: 30 HP
  2. Damage Taken: 25 points
    • HP after damage: 30 - 25 = 5 HP
  3. New Exhaustion Level
    • Hit point maximum is halved: 30 ÷ 2 = 15 HP
    • Current HP remains at 5 (as it's already below the new maximum)
  4. Remaining HP before unconsciousness:
    • The character's current HP is 5
    • They will fall unconscious at 0 HP
    • Therefore, they can take 5 more points of damage before falling unconscious

In this case, the character can receive 5 more points of damage before falling unconscious. The reduction in hit point maximum due to exhaustion doesn't affect the character's current HP in this scenario because their HP was already below the new maximum.

Conclusion

  • If you have less damage than half your max HP, then you're currently at half your max HP.
  • If you have as much or more damage than half your max HP, then the exhaustion has no effect.

Diagonal Movement

Optional Rule is used. The first diagonal square counts as 5 feet, but the second diagonal square counts as 10 feet13. This pattern of 5 feet and 10 feet continues for subsequent diagonal movements.

Ranged Attacks

  • Should the path of a ranged attack against a target cross another single creatures, it counts as half cover.
  • Should the path of a ranged attack against a target cross 2 or more creature, it counts as three-quarters cover.

Encumbrance

This will be discussed at session 0.

Hiding

Means a character is both unseen and unheard by any opposing character. You can't hide from a creature that can see you. If you make noise, you give away your position.

Material Spell Components

You do not have to track material spell components unless there is a gold piece value listed.

Example, Chromatic Orb lists "diamond worth at least 50 gp". You must be hold - or whatever spell description states what to do with component - the diamond worth at least 50 gp to cast Chromatic Orb.
