Schedule and Attendance

Unless there is medical issue, an emergency, or I am out of town, a session will be scheduled every other week starting with Session 0 held January 30th, 2025. Except for Session 0 and any session I identify at least 2 weeks ahead of time your attendance is not required but definitely encouraged.

If there are only 3 players:

  • the missing player’s PC is sidelined.
  • an NPC controlled by a player with GM override can join the party

If there are only 2 players, the group can decide to either play boardgames or proceed where:

  • the missing players’ PCs are sidelined.
  • 2 NPCs controlled by the players by with override can join the party

Hopefully the following will not occur but I want to give fair warning. In the event that I should experience a depressive episode, rather than cancel the session, boardgames will played, RPGs will be discussed, it doesn’t have to this campaign. Could be something you’re interested in running. Talking RPGs gets me motivated. And therefore hopefully back on track.

Published Sources

I don’t think I need to say this to this group but just to be perfectly clear, mfy greatest joy in RPGs is giving out clues and watching the players try to work out what it all means. I also use published materials in my campaigns. It’s one thing if I use something you’re already familiar with but completely different if you go looking. You’re taking away my fun. So please don’t.

My Definition of Small Stakes Lawful Evil

In high school a friend and a friend of his, who we’ll call Karl, played in a one shot D&D 1e game I ran. Karl brought his own character that was in another DM’s campaign. Karl explained that anything that happened to his PC in my game would transfer over to the other DM’s campaign. I responded “As long as the other DM is OK with it, it’s fine with me.” Although, I wondered if he died, would he have told his DM?

I ran B5 Horror on the Hill. I didn’t know until years later that Karl knew I was going to run B5 and he talked to someone who owned it and learned some key points such as where to find a ring of invisibility.

So during the game Karl’s PC sneaks off by himself and literally trips on the body wearing the ring of invisibility. Apparently in his mind since acquired the ring while in played despite in my book cheating, he followed the rules, and all was good. However, if he had simply wrote on his character sheet ring of invisibility that would have broken the rules and therefore been bad. I would argue that "following his rules" was worse because it robbed the other players the oppotunity to claim that ring. Precious!

Anyway, due to other shenanigans by Karl, by the end adventure he pissed off Brian and two other players so much that they came to me and said they wanted to pool their money together and hire an assassin to kill Karl’s PC. Of course Brian was aware that the first edition Dungeon Master’s had a table to determine the percentage chance of an assassin|Assassination Table of a given level to kill a character of a given level. They wanted me to roll d100 and insta-kill Karl’s PC. Ha good times!
