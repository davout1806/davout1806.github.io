Frost Touched

Beginning at 1st level, you learn the Ray Of Frost can trip.

Channel Divinity: Frost Armor

Starting at 2nd level, you can use your Channel Divinity to protect yourself with a hard layer of semitransparent permafrost, which grants you resistance to cold damage and a +2 bonus to Armor Class for one hour.

Palefoot

Beginning at 6th level, you ignore difficult terrain made of ice or snow. Also, while in cold weather, your speed increases by 10 feet and you gain a climb speed equal to your normal movement speed.

Cold Hands

At 8th level, you gain the ability to infuse your weapon strikes with the icy power of your divine patron. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal an extra 1d8 cold damage to the target. When you reach 14th level, the extra damage increases to 2d8.

Channel Divinity: Elemental Ice

Upon reaching 17th level, you can use your Channel Divinity to transform into living ice. For the next minute, you are immune to cold damage, gain a +4 bonus to Armor Class, and you can cast any spell from the frost domain at its lowest level without using a spell slot. Once you use this power, you must finish a short rest before using it again.

Fluff

Survival in cold conditions is a hallmark adaptation of many intelligent species. In particular, some cultures have found it advantageous to revere this dangerous element of nature the same way many revere fire or storms, mountains or light.

Priests of the frost are stoic, pragmatic, for they have little to waste when survival hangs in such precarious balance against the primal forces of an unforgiving god.
