1st Level: Bonus Proficiencies

Beginning when you choose this domain at 1st leveL you gain proficiency in martial ranged weapons, and you become proficient in the Perception skill

1st Level: Archer's Luck

Starting at 1st leve~ if you hit a creature with a ranged weapon attack, you can choose to make the hit a critical hit.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

2nd Level: Channel Divinity: Bowman's Guile

Starting at 2nd leve~ you can use your Channel Divinity to imbue yourself with incredible speed and focus.

As an action, you can use your Channel Divinity to become blessed with the swiftness and resolve of a master archer. For 1 minute, your walking speed increases by 10 feet, and once per turn when you miss with a ranged weapon attack, you can repeat the attack roll against the same target.

6th Level: Steady Eye

Starting at 6th leveL your mastery of archery and perception grants you the following benefits:

  • Attacking prone creatures or at long range doesn't impose disadvantage on your ranged weapon attack rolls.
  • An area being lightly obscured doesn't impose disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

8th Level: Divine Strike

You gain the ability to infuse your weapon strikes with divine energy. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal an extra 1d8 damage to the target. The extra damage is of the same type normally dealt by the weapon. When you reach 14th level, the extra damage increases to 2d8.

17th Level: Mastery Of Bow & Arrow

Starting at 17th leveL you have advantage on all ranged weapon attack rolls.

Fluff

The gods of archery - including Ullr, Hou Yi, Rama, Artemis, Hayk, Osoosi, and Skadi - embody precision, self reliance, and patience. Some of these gods are also gods of hunting and survivaL and value perseverance and selfsufficiency. Others teach that the bow is an extension of one's self, and thus practicing with bows becomes both a physical and spiritual endeavor. Some gods of archery also preside over war or sportsmanship, watching over followers who wield the bow for combat and competition. Followers of these gods study and practice with bows daily, focusing on hunting, warfare, or sport.
