1st Level: Bonus Proficiency

When you choose this domain at first level, you gain proficiency with the Deception skill.

1st Level: In Plain Sight

You have Advantage against any ability, spell, or power that allows a saving throw or other check to prevent detection of your alignment.

1st Level: That’s a Great Idea

Also at first level, you have the ability to sway others to your cause. A number of times a day equal to your Charisma modifier +1 (minimum 1), you can cast the Friends spell. At the end of the spell you may make a Deception check (DC = 10 + targets level). If successful, the creature doesn’t realize it was influenced. If you fail, the normal affects of the spell apply.

2nd Level: Channel Divinity: I Don’t Even Know That Guy

Starting at 2nd level you can use your Channel Divinity to focus blame on someone or something else. Make a Deception check (your DM will set the difficulty – typically 10 or 15) and for every point you succeed, you convince another person that it wasn’t you. Work with your GM to figure out bonuses based on circumstance and levels of success based on people convinced. Examples include planting evidence or bribing witnesses.

6th Level: What Spell? I Didn’t Cast a Spell

At 6th level you gain the ability to hide your spell casting. Damage inflicting spells, unless explicitly stated in the rules, can be made to look they are coming from another being or direction. You roll Deception against a difficulty of 10 + Spell Level to succeed. For example, Spiritual Weapon can look like it is coming from another caster or Guiding Bolt could be coming from a different direction. This ability can also be used to disguise the use of Sacred Flame.

8th Level: No, That is Not What I Said

At 8th level, add your Wisdom Modifier to your Deception checks.

Fluff

It’s not enough to just say that you’re evil; you need to back it up. World domination, destruction of a country or way of life, genocide, extinction on a global scale-big plans mean evil on a grand scale. Working in the background to sway others to your despicable cause is your preferred method of operation. A Cleric with the Evil Domain doesn’t need an evil deity, just an overriding evil purpose. A really good evil laugh doesn’t hurt either...
