Item Retrieval Agent System

Step 1: Hiring an Agent

Players must first hire an agent who specializes in acquiring rare items. Agents vary in skill and reliability, which affects both success rate and search duration. The cost listed here is just the fee for the agent. The price of the the item is extra.

  • Low-Tier Agent (Cheap & Unreliable) Costs 10 gp per week, -10% to success rate
  • Standard Agent (Competent & Reliable) Costs 25 gp per week, no penalty
  • Elite Agent (Highly Skilled & Well-Connected) Costs 50 gp per week, +10% to success rate

Players pay the agent's weekly fee regardless of success unless they negotiate otherwise.

Step 2: Determining Success

Each item has a rarity level from 1 (common) to 6 (legendary). Rarer items are harder to find.

Base Success Rates by Rarity

  • Level 1 (common) 90%
  • Level 2 (uncommon) 75%
  • Level 3 (rare) 50%
  • Level 4 (very rare) 25%

Roll 1d100 and compare to success rate (modified by agent type).

  • If the roll is equal to or lower than the success rate, the item is found
  • If the roll is higher, the agent fails but must still be paid

Agent Modifiers

  • Low-Tier Agent -10% to success chance
  • Elite Agent +10% to success chance

Other possible modifiers

  • Connections If the agent has ties to a relevant faction (+5% to success)
  • Player Bribes or Persuasion Players can use Persuasion, Deception, or Intimidation (DC 15) to get an extra +5% bonus
  • Dangerous Item If the item is heavily guarded or in an enemy’s possession (-10% success)

Step 3: Determining Search Duration

The more rare an item, the longer it takes to find.

Rarity LevelTimeframeRoll Modifier
Level 1 (common)7-10 days1d4 + 6
Level 2 (uncommon)16-30 days2d8 + 14
Level 3 (rare)1-3 months1d60 + 30 (days)
Level 4 (very rare)3-6 months1d100 + 85 (days)

If an Elite Agent is used, reduce the time by 10%. If a Low-Tier Agent is used, increase the time by 10%.

Step 4: Costs and Risks

Costs

  • The cost listed is just the fee for the agent. The price of the the item is extra.
  • The agent is paid per week, whether or not they find the item
  • Players can negotiate to pay extra upfront (double weekly fee) to increase the agent’s effort (+5% success)
  • If an item is dangerous to acquire, the agent may demand hazard pay (double the normal rate for a duration)

Failure Consequences

  • If the agent fails, they may return with a lead instead (e.g., "I found a seller, but they won’t part with it unless you do a favor")
  • Dishonest agents might scam the players, providing a fake item (Insight DC 15 to detect)
  • A failed roll of 95-100 could mean the agent disappears, is arrested, or killed, depending on the DM’s discretion

Alternative: Player Involvement

  • Players can track down multiple agents, making multiple rolls simultaneously (expensive, but increases odds of success)
  • Favors Instead of Payment. Some agents may ask for favors instead of gold, leading to additional side quests
