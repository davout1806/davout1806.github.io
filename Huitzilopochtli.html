(weet-see-loh-POHK-tlee)

Huitzilopochtli is the god of war. His translated name „Left-handed Hummingbird‰ is deceiving, for he is a fierce, bloodthirsty god. He was born fully-grown of a mortal woman, wearing blue jade armor and armed with a bright red axe. He resembles a blue-skinned man with hummingbird feathers decorating his head and left leg.

This god may appear on any battlefield where his worshipers are fighting and aid them, but this will not be in such a manner as to make sure his side will win. When the god chooses to appear for a battle, he will take the body of a dead warrior who won great victories either during that battle or in the past. No matter what the outcome of the battle (i.e., win, lose, or draw), that warrior will be raised and will live a long and lucky life.

Huitzilopochtli's clerics must embody the spirit of war. They must always be willing to fight without regard for their own lives. Their primary duty is presiding over the deaths of prisoners taken in Huitzilopochtli's name. Sacrifices are made to him only in battle and only by fighting clerics. He can rarely be called upon for any non-warlike situation and can never be communed with.
