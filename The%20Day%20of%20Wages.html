Origins and Purpose

The Day of Wages is a sacred festival dedicated to Zilchus, the Oeridian god of merchants, business, power, and fair commerce. It celebrates the honest accumulation of wealth and the importance of integrity in trade. Tradition holds that this day marks the anniversary of Zilchus’ legendary first trade, when he exchanged wisdom for prosperity and established the divine laws of commerce.

It is said that in the earliest days of civilization, merchants toiled without guidance, preyed upon by thieves and deceived by their own greed. Zilchus, taking the form of a wise guildmaster, introduced the principles of fairness, contract law, and honorable trade. The Day of Wages is thus a time for merchants and laborers alike to reflect on the virtue of just compensation and mutual prosperity.
