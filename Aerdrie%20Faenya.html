(AYR-dree FIN-yuh)

Aerdrie appears to be a tall, elf-like woman with feathered hair and eyebrows. From her back spring a pair of large, birdlike wings. Her feathers are of constantly changing color. The lower half of her body from the hips down vanishes into a misty whirlwind, so that she appears to never touch the ground. Aerdrie wanders the winds of the planes of Olympus and Gladsheim, in the company of a large number of winged creatures of many types.

If aroused for battle, Aerdrie is able to cast great blasts of wind against any opponent. She may also summon air elementals, giant eagles, or rocs. These creatures will obey her unto death. This goddess is able to use all spells involving air, weather, flight, electricity, and gas as often as desired. She has the power to negate at will the flying or levitating abilities of any creature.

Any elf of non-lawful and non-evil alignment may worship Aerdrie. Elves who desire certain weather conditions make the most frequent sacrifices to her, and her cult is popular with elves who possess flying mounts (griffons, hippogriffs, pegasi, and so forth). It has also been known to happen that an elf who has somehow fallen from a great height, should he or she call out Aerdrie's name aloud, has a small chance of receiving a feather fall spell just prior to striking the ground. However, those who receive this gracious benefit, coming directly from Aerdrie herself, will be both quested and geased to perform a service for the goddess. Such tasks vary widely in nature, though all of them are said to be comparatively challenging and difficult. Elves receiving this benefit of a feather fall need not be worshipers of Aerdrie, but should be of the appropriate alignment.

Exploration and maintaining good relations with avian races (e.g., giant eagles and aarakocra) are prime duties for Aerdrie's clerics. Development of musical skills on wind instruments and acquiring magical aids to flight are also important goals. Novices of Aerdrie (levels 1-2) are known as Eaglets or the Tethered. Full priests (level 3+) are known as Winged Brothers or Sisters.
