1st Level: Glimpse

When you choose this domain at 1st level, you periodically receive momentary glimpses of possible futures, which can give you an edge in certain situations. You gain the Guidance and Resistance cantrips. You may also add your Wisdom modifier to your initiative rolls.

2nd Level: Channel Divinity: Pierce The Veil

Starting at 2nd level, you can use your Channel Divinity to look beyond the physical world and into the complex pattern which underlies all of reality, thus helping you determine the true nature of a situation. As an action, you may spend a use of your Channel Divinity to gain advantage to all Perception. Investigation, and Insight rolls for 10 minutes.

6th Level: Fate Foretold

At 6th level you learn how to view the twisted skeins of fate and use that knowledge to your advantage. When you finish a long or short rest, roll 2d20. referred to as fate dice, pick one of them, then record the number it rolled.

When you or a creature that you can see makes an attack roll saving throw, or ability check, you may declare that the number you recorded from the fate die is the result of the roll instead of actually rolling. Treat the result as though the recorded number had been rolled on the die.

When you finish a long or short rest, you lose any unused fate dice.

8th Level: Potent Spellcasting

Starting at 8th level, you add your Wisdom modifier to the damage you deal with any cleric cantrip.

17th Level: Master Of Fate

Beginning at 17th level when you roll fate dice for your Fate Foretold feature, you may roll4d20 instead of 2d20. You may pick two of them and record the numbers they rolled. You may use these for your Fate Foretold feature aa normal. but you may select either fate die when you do so. You may not use more than one fate die per turn, and you may use each fate die only once.

Fluff

The Fate domain belongs to gods who concern themselves with prophecy predetermination, and inevitability. To these gods, reality itself has a pattern, and all of existence falls within that grand design. Each deity and their various churches envision the overarching plan and pattern to reality in a different way. As they see it, each point of view is just a different perspective on the same painting. grand machine, or tapestry. The future is written just as indelibly as the past and the present, and as such, it can be understood with enough study and insight.

The faithful of these deities are often regarded as prophets, soothsayers, seers, or fortune tellers, and the truly devoted are granted powers of insight and prescience by their deities, so it often falls on them to guide their friends when the way forward is unclear. The spells and blessings of these clerics usually manifest in the form of divinely inspired foreknowledge or prophecy that allows the cleric and his allies to more effectively move forward as they explore their fates.

While this knowledge may not always provide an exact map, it often points in the right direction or reveals clues that might otherwise go unnoticed Sometimes it gives warning enough to avoid an axe's blade or a dragon's breath. A fate cleric's spells can even be used to obfuscate or deceive other powers who may be trying to read the weave of fate, effectively shielding the target from detection as it seems to temporarily disappear from the grand design.
