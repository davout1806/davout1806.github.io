(LAH-duh-gwer)

Grim and gloomy, this joyless god long ago forsook his brothers in the dwarven pantheon and exiled himself to the fringe dwarven communities who were the ancestors of the duergar. Laduguer's nature is certainly evilly inclined, but much of this is the evil of a being turned in on itself and bitter at what he sees as being unvalued and rejected by the other dwarven gods. Laduguer is a supremely lawful deity, unbending and harsh, and he despises the other dwarven gods as lazy, indolent, and feckless.

Laduguer is a slave driver who demands constant toil under harsh conditions from his people. Nevertheless, he does reward hard work, by teaching the crafting of magical items (especially weapons) and by extending his protective powers. He will defend a hard-working and oppressed duergar community by use of protective and warding magic, rarely entering into open battle. Laduguer appears as a tall, gaunt duergar with skin coloring that can change from gray to brown shades to match his environment. He is bald and always wears a frown.
