Our Heros

  • Delvon Brightwood – Half-elf Paladin
  • Flint Sunsong – Dwarf Monk
  • Gimblebok – Gnome Druid
  • Gwador – Half-orc Fighter

Planting 13th, 576 CY

I don't recall the order because once again I forgot to hit record.

  • Investigated the missing Golden Gate Lynx Statue. Determined it was taked in an initiation rite of the Black Fists a group of mostly guards and a few civilians who mostly gathered to drink and bitch.
  • Investigated the Rumor by placing money in the tree and staking it out. At night discovered a lone member from Rikar's Ravens showed up to collect the the money. After being captured, he informed the party that it was all a scam. The rumor was made up to put fear on superstitious people, and collect easy money.
    • After much discussion, Gwador untied the Raven and let him go.
  • Flint got a note from a Tormin Greyhaft that stated:
    • Draeligor was a dour warrior, single-minded and determined, passing through, speaking little of his quest. Tormin believes he was injured when he came through, headed towards the mine. Never saw him again.
  • Flint met with Khemog Mountaingut, a former prospector, who some claimed was half-mad or drunk. He said:
    • Draeligor last name was Vhennax.
    • “Saw 'im. Tall. Pale as marble. Wore silence like a cloak. Carved in the deep tunnels with a knife that shimmered—no chisel. Just etched with light. Didn’t speak to me. Just looked up once, eyes like old stone—sad, like he’d lost something long before he came here. I told the others. They laughed. Said I drank too deep o' the Still Ale. Maybe I did. But the words he carved? I can still see ‘em.”
  • One morning Delvon found Gwador reading elvish poetry.
  • Flint received word that Greytar has some information regarding the Fist of the Earth-Forged.
