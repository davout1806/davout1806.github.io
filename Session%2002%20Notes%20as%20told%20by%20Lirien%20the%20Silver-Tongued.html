The Ballad of Frandor’s Keep: Shadows Beneath the Stone

By the wandering bard, Lirien the Silver-Tongued

A Woman’s Plea and the Thugs in the Road

On the tenth day of Planting, when the morning mist still clung to the earth like a ghost’s whisper, a woman sought out our heroes at Frandor’s Keep. She called herself Leona Brast, and her eyes held the weight of fear. Her husband, she claimed, had vanished—a merchant’s debt had drawn him onto the Old Has Road, and he had not returned. With a desperate voice, she implored them for aid.

Without hesitation, the band set out, their boots stirring the dust of the well-worn path. Yet, fate wove its cruel threads into their journey, for as they neared the fateful intersection of the Borderland Road, shadows burst forth from the roadside brambles. Five thugs, hard-eyed and ill-intentioned, closed in, weapons glinting in the dappled light.

“A message from Jiric Helsan,” their leader sneered, steel ringing as it was drawn. “He says you need a lesson about minding your own damn business.”

But our heroes were not ones to cower before brute force. Blades met flesh, and fists struck bone as battle was joined. It was a vicious struggle, blood staining the ground as three of the attackers fell where they stood. The last two, battered and beaten, surrendered.

Yet the victors had not fought alone. From the shifting foliage at the roadside emerged an unexpected ally—a man clad in robes of deep azure, the holy symbol of Trithereon gleaming at his throat. His name was Goazaar, a priest of the Avenger, and he was no stranger to Helsan’s cruelty.

“I’ve crossed paths with that filth before,” he murmured, glancing at the fallen. “I imagine our paths will cross again.” Goazaar’s news was grave. Randric, the ever-wily guide, had vanished the night before, last seen near the graveyard. And darker still, Quarrytown—ruled in all but name by the enigmatic Rikar—held within its seedy heart a den of misery: The Den of Delights, where souls were bought and sold. He asked for the party’s aid in freeing those trapped within.

The heroes, though weary, could not ignore such a call. But first, there was another mystery to chase.

The Tomb Beneath the Keep

Returning to Frandor’s Keep, they sought the graveyard where Randric had last been seen. There, beneath the solemn watch of the Keep’s stone walls, they met Shajani, a wanderer of the dead’s domain, who spoke of restless spirits haunting the night.

Something was amiss. A grave, unearthed, yawned open before them, and tracks told of frantic movement between it and a hidden passage—a tunnel, leading beneath the Keep itself. Without hesitation, they descended.

The darkness swallowed them whole, and in that subterranean silence, they found the stench of death waiting. In the gloom, something moved—something once a man, now a thing of hunger and rot. A ghoul lunged, its hollow eyes fixed upon them with an unnatural yearning. Steel and spell brought it low, and when the dust of battle settled, they pieced together its tragic tale.

It had once been Vos Wilkers, a soldier, a man of the Keep. And unlike the nameless dead, he had been buried instead of burned. A sign of favor? Or a secret left to fester beneath the stone?

Randric’s fate was clearer, and darker still. His lifeless body lay crumpled in the shadows. His story, though ended, left behind a trail of riddles: (to be clear the skull is not Randric's, his body still had his.)

  • A skull missing its two front teeth.
  • A lockbox containing a letter that swore Sir Aedric Vane was no traitor.
  • A ring, inscribed with love.
  • Platinum coins, their letters reversed.
  • A flute, carved with care.

And a mystery carved in stone—the list of the Keep’s Knight-Commanders bore one name, chiseled away, a deliberate erasure. The name could have been Vane’s, stricken from history’s ledgers like a sin unspoken.

Their hands were heavy with burden as they returned Randric’s body to his mother, placing his earnings in her trembling hands. It was a small kindness, but kindness nonetheless.

And yet, the mystery of Leona Brast remained. Seeking answers, they turned to the carpenters of the Keep. Mal Varasin, master of wood and nail, and his journeyman, Feranis, had never heard the name. A dead end, or something deeper?

Into the Shadows of Quarrytown

The road twisted toward Quarrytown, where the law of Frandor’s Keep held no weight. Here, coin was king, and the shadows whispered with unseen tongues. To move through its streets unchallenged, one must first pay their due, and so the party paid their “tax” at the Pavilion before making their way to the Toe Hold, the kind of place where a man could drown his worries—or be drowned by them.

Among the low-lit tables, the eyes of the underworld watched. Kiparus Belan, a dwarf with unknown dealings. Two nameless souls, their conversations dipped in intrigue. And there, slouched in a corner like a spider in its web, was Jiric Helsan.

Gimblebok, clever as ever, worked his magic—not with spell or steel, but with nature’s smallest agent. A whisper in the right ears, a promise of sweet grain, and soon a legion of mice swarmed the slaver’s legs, their tiny claws scrabbling for the feast that did not exist.

Jiric leapt from his seat, cursing and swatting, as the tide of rodents sent him fleeing from the tavern.

The mice, displeased at the deception, chattered their complaints, but Gimblebok, ever generous, placated them with rations.

As drink flowed and words loosened, the whispers of Quarrytown revealed their secrets: * A murder, a pair of men fleeing into the woods, and the Old Mine that might hide them. * The Old Mine itself, rumored to be haunted by spirits... or perhaps something worse. * A man-wolf, lurking within its depths.

Meanwhile, Gwador and Flint sought the Den of Delights, their eyes and ears open to its sorrow. One among its captives, Kibata, spoke with bitterness hardened by despair. She had heard the false promises before—promises of freedom that led only to disappointment.

“Where would I go?” she asked, her voice a blade dulled by time.

And the heroes, though full of fire, had no answer. Not yet.

The Beast Below the Earth

Dawn saw them on the road once more, their path leading toward the Old Mine. The wind carried the scent of damp stone and old secrets as they neared the yawning maw of the cavern.

And at its threshold, a guardian awaited—a bear, ancient and wise in its own way. Gimblebok, speaking in the tongue of beasts, wove words between them.

The bear had seen nothing larger than itself pass this way. But something had come and gone—something man-sized, something that screamed.

And deeper still, the mine breathed. A gassy stench clung to the air, and the darkness yawned before them, whispering of what lay in its depths.

The man-wolf, if it existed, awaited below.

And so, with steel drawn and hearts steeled, they stepped forward into the abyss.

The Gathering Storm

Beneath Frandor’s Keep, secrets stir. In Quarrytown, chains wait to be broken. And in the depths of the Old Mine, something watches.

Jiric Helsan is not done with them. Rikar’s shadow looms ever larger. And the mysteries of Aedric Vane, the buried past, and the fate of those enslaved call to them.

The road ahead is dark.

But they do not walk it alone.
