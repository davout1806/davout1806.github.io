In the dark jungles and lost temples of the southern Flanaess and Hepmonaland, whispered tales tell of Camazotz, the Vampire Bat God of the Olman pantheon — a nightmarish deity of blood, death, and fear. Unlike the more balanced or benevolent nature deities of other cultures, Camazotz represents the primordial terror of the night: a god of predation, sacrifice, and shadows who hungers not only for blood but for the very lifeforce of mortals. Among the Olman peoples, particularly those who fell into darkness and decadence, he was once revered as a bringer of divine judgment and a keeper of sacrificial balance, his worship fueled by fear rather than love. Like many Olman gods, he has a dual aspect — the ravenous monster that spreads plague and sickness as well as the lord of the the benign fliers that eat crop-destroying insects. It is possible that Camazotz gained popularity (or notoriety) due to the ancient bat-people who used to live in Hepmonaland.

His temples, hidden in crumbling step pyramids or carved into cliffside caves, were places of bloodletting and ritual sacrifice, where his priests, cloaked in flayed bat hides, enacted grim rites to appease his hunger. Camazotz’s faithful believe that only blood quenches divine thirst, and they offer lives — animal or human — in exchange for power, protection, or transformation. Some even seek undeath through his favor, becoming vampire-spawn-like beings who serve his will.

Despite being a relatively obscure deity in the broader world, Camazotz's influence lingers like a diseas, often in secretive death cults, vampire covens, or isolated Olman remnants who cling to ancient, dreadful rites. His name is invoked to inspire fear, and his symbol, a giant descending vampire bat, marks places of slaughter and forbidden power.

Today, adventurers exploring ancient Olman ruins or delving into forgotten tombs may still stumble upon Camazotz’s sigils, long-dead priests preserved by unholy rites, or worse, echoes of the god himself, hungering in the dark between forgotten altars and the dreaming underworld.

Camazotz is primarily a solitary god, staying out of the machinations of other deities. Occasionally, he will make temporary alliances with those willing to pay him in blood to enact vicious attacks on their enemies. His only true allies are various demon lords with whom he has associated with in the past. His primary enemy is Quetzalcoatl, whose followers he has come into conflict with many times.

Manifestations

Camazotz (or Zotzilaha, as he is known by the Olman people dwelling in the Amedio) is most often portrayed as a monstrous, humanoid bat with eyes like coals and wings that blot out the stars. He is said to haunt the caverns of the earth and the moonless skies, preying on the unwary and the wicked alike. His body is covered in coarse, dark fur, his humanoid features are lithe and muscular, suggestive of his skill as a predator. Camazotz’s hands have fingers that extend into spindly claws, and the leathery wings spreading from each of his arms allows him to take flight at a moment’s notice. He sports digitigrade legs, and his feet each bear a trio of claws he uses to hang upside-down from ceilings, much like his bat kin. Camazotz frequently manifests himself as a great bat surrounded by a cloud made up of thousands of normal bats. Blood dripping from wall carvings and eyes watching from the darkness are frequently interpreted as signs of displeasure from his servants and his faithful.

The Church

  • CLERGY: Clerics, Bloodkeepers, shamans
  • CLERGY’S ALIGNMENT: LE, NE, CE

The Church of Camazotz, feared and forgotten by most in the modern Flanaess, once cast a long and terrible shadow over the ancient Olman civilization. Worship of Camazotz, the vampire bat god of blood, darkness, and sacrifice, centered on the belief that life must be fed to death so that the balance of the cosmos might be preserved through the god’s eternal hunger. Cloaked in silence and ritual, his church was as much a cult of fear as it was of faith.

At its height, the Church of Camazotz operated from massive stone pyramids carved deep within the jungle, each a temple and tomb. These sanctuaries were built near cave mouths or natural chasms — places the Olman believed connected to the underworld. His priests, known as Bloodkeepers, wore robes of jet-black feathers or bat leather, often adorned with bones and crimson-dyed sashes. Their faces were masked in bat-like visages carved from obsidian or decorated skulls, and their rites were performed at night, by torchlight and beneath new moons.

Central to the church was the practice of blood sacrifice, both of animals and, more often, captives taken in war or chosen by lot. Blood was spilled into sacred pools or onto stone altars carved to resemble bat wings, and Camazotz was said to whisper his will through swarms of bats or through the mouths of his vampire high priests. Some rituals were designed not merely to appease the god, but to transform his faithful into undead servants, embracing his cursed gift in the form of vampirism or other shadow-tainted mutations.

The church maintained a strict hierarchy based on power, secrecy, and blood. Advancement came only through service, sacrifice, and survival of dark rites. Camazotz’s clergy valued stealth, cruelty, and control, often operating in the shadows even within larger Olman religious systems. In times of Olman decline, the church grew even more powerful, feeding on fear and chaos as civilization crumbled around them.

In modern times, the Church of Camazotz survives only in scattered cults, often hidden deep in jungle ruins, Underdark enclaves, or among vampiric lineages that trace their cursed blood to ancient rituals. Adventurers who uncover one of these forgotten shrines may find altars still stained with sacrificial blood and hear the echo of leathery wings in the blackness above.

Dogma: Blood is power in Camazotz’s eyes. Blood holds life and strength. To drink blood is to sustain oneself and grow. Camazotz’s decrees are simple: prey on others and keep sacred both the night and predators. The hunt is of foremost importance to Camazotz, but unlike those who hunt for sustenance or sport, Camazotz values hunting purely for the sake of killing. Brutality is the key to a favored hunt, and Camazotz encourages his followers to delight in the spilling of blood that happens at the end of a successful kill. Blood stands in as a measure of a predator’s skill, and every drop spilled in his name earns greater favor from the god.

Day-to-Day Activities

What little structure exists in Camazotz’s religion takes the form of hunts and rituals. Occasionally, an individual might take a leadership role among a group of followers, organizing hunts and performing sacrifices dressed in skins stripped from the cult’s victims. Most worshippers can hunt and make sacrifices on their own, however, so a formal position like a priest is rarely needed. Larger groups might have one or two prominent members, but mostly for the sake of keeping other worshippers in line. Violence is common among Camazotz’s bloodthirsty followers, and leaders tend to be the strongest members of the group. This authority allows them to break up fights or slay potentially disruptive members. As strength is the ultimate sign of a capable predator, it’s not surprising that worshippers of Camazotz often fight each other for dominance or to win the favor of their god.

Holy Days & Important Ceremonies

Camazotzi worshippers are rarely organized enough to observe many regular standard holidays, but two events are recognized by most followers of the Lord of Stolen Blood.

Bloodletting Night: This violent celebration of Camazotz’s will occurs every winter solstice, when the year’s longest night provides the opportunity for the longest hunt as well. During the celebration, Camazotz’s worshippers slay as many victims as possible, spilling the blood of their hunts wherever they go, though some victims are captured alive to provide nourishment for future rituals. Most of these rituals occur shortly after Bloodletting Night, ensuring that the blood of these captives remains fresh.

Mooncall: The night of a full moon is sacred to Camazotz. In addition to being advantageous for hunting, nights of the full moon are when Camazotz’s werecreature followers transform into their most powerful selves. Camazotzi werecreatures make good use of the night, hunting and terrorizing to their full potential. Adherents who aren’t werecreatures often use the night to spread fear or offer sacrifices to Camazotz, but some seek out werecreatures in an attempt to receive the curse of the werecreature through their bites. Among the worshippers of Camazotz, Mooncall is also a time for internal conflict. Though most of this violence is consensual, many would-be Camazotzi leaders use the night to prove themselves by overthrowing the current leader of their respective group.

Major Centers of Worship

Camazotz maintains few formal temples. Most of his places of worship are little more than impromptu shrines lashed together from the bones of prey and tucked into caves. Camazotz’s followers usually perform their rituals in caverns, though they occasionally worship in glades or open fields. When this occurs, it’s most often on nights of the full moon. The most formal ceremonies take place around statues of Camazotz, during which followers bathe in blood as part of their sacrifices. Sometimes, the faithful even create statues with particularly sharp fangs, allowing them to sacrifice victims directly inside the statue’s mouth. Camazotz generally discourages the creation of more permanent structures, as such things begin to resemble civilization. The Lord of Stolen Blood abhors the comfort civilization brings, finding that it sours the blood. He prefers his followers to keep to natural locales for worship.

Affiliated Orders

Among the dark folds of Camazotz's faith, a few infamous affiliated orders served the vampire bat god's cruel will, chief among them the Crimson Echo and the Wings of the Last Night. The Crimson Echo is a secretive order of assassins and blood-priests who believe that silent, surgical sacrifice is the highest devotion to Camazotz; they move unseen, leaving victims exsanguinated and marked with the bat god’s bloody sigil. In contrast, the Wings of the Last Night are a fanatical warrior order composed of blood-bound undead and vampiric initiates, chosen for their devotion and “blessed” through dark rites. They guard sacred jungle temples and execute mass sacrifices during rare lunar conjunctions. Both orders operate independently yet align in their ultimate purpose: to bring about an age of red twilight, where Camazotz's wings blot out the sun and the world drinks only blood. These orders exist now only as shadows of their former selves, but intrepid travelers into the depths of the Amedio or Hepmonaland would do well to watch for any signs of their continued existence.

Priestly Vestments

Priests of Camazotz wore robes of jet-black feathers or bat leather, often adorned with bones and crimson-dyed sashes. Their faces were masked in bat-like visages carved from obsidian or decorated skulls, and their rites were performed at night, by torchlight and beneath new moons.
