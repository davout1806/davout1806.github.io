1st Level: Blessings of Youth

When you choose this domain at 1st level, you become proficient in one musical instrument, one set of artisan’s tools or another musical instrument, and two skills of your choice, from acrobatics, athletics, deception, performance, or persuasion.

Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check that uses either of the skills you chose, or that uses a tool or instrument you chose as part of this feature.

1st Level: Unarmored Defense

Also starting at 1st level, while you are wearing no armor, your armor class is equal to 10 + your Charisma modifier + your Dexterity modifier.

2nd Level: Channel Divinity: Frenzy of Youth

Starting at 2nd level, you can use your Channel Divinity to inspire a brief, ecstatic frenzy state in others.

As an action, designate a number of creatures of your choice up to your Wisdom modifier within 30 feet. Each of the subjects may, on their next turn, dodge, dash, or make a melee weapon attack as a bonus action, or alternately, make a Strength or Dexterity check with advantage.

Those who benefit from this ability may also choose to ignore one level of exhaustion for up to one hour, though they gain another level of exhaustion at the end of the hour if they do so.

6th Level: Aura of Youth

Beginning at 6th level, whenever you roll initiative or use your Channel Divinity, all creatures of your choice within 30 feet may choose to spend one hit die. If they do so, they gain temporary hit points equal to the maximum roll for that die instead of gaining hit points.

8th Level: Divine Strike

At 8th level, you gain the ability to infuse your weapon strikes with divine energy. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal an extra 1d8 radiant damage to the target. When you reach 14th level, the extra damage increases to 2d8.

17th Level: Youthful Energy

Starting at 17th level, you and up to six creatures within 30 feet may gain the benefits of a short rest and remove all levels of exhaustion by engaging in one minute of dance or similarly vigorous physical activity. Creatures with no exhaustion to remove who spend hit dice may choose to re-roll any or all of the hit dice they spend, keeping the second result even if it is lower than the first.

To gain these benefits, you and anyone who receives them must concentrate on the activity as they would a spell for the entire time. Once you have successfully used this ability, you may not use it again until you have completed a long rest. A given creature cannot benefit from this feature more than once between long rests.

Fluff

The gods are almost by definition ageless, which lends special significance to the apparent age of any form they choose to represent themselves. A god who habitually appears stooped and gray might represent maturity and Wisdom, or the end of a cycle. Gods who seem young and vibrant often represent vitality and the exuberant energy of life. Some very few such gods are embodiments of youth itself in all its joyous, carefree cruelty.

The domain of youth is closely related to the domain of life. Deities of youth are often the children of gods of life or nature. They represent life at its undiminished peak.

The youth domain may also be used to represent the portfolios of gods of intoxication or the season of spring.
