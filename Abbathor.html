(AB-ah-thawr)

At times, Abbathor has been the ally of the other dwarf gods, but his treachery and lust for treasure make him a dangerous associate. He will never help any non-dwarf deity or creature. He cannot be trusted to do anything but evil, unless it suits his own purposes to give assistance. Abbathor was not always as devoted to Evil as he is now. Originally, his sphere of influence concerned the appreciation of valuable gems and metals, not necessarily at the expense of others. He became embittered when Moradin appointed Dumathoin the protector of the mountain dwarf instead of him, and from that day forward has become ever more devious and self-serving in his endeavors, in a continual effort to wreak revenge on the other gods by establishing greed · especially evil greed · as the driving force in dwarf lives. His home caverns are said to be made of purest gold, which may be a fact or may be a legend born of his reputation for hoarding that which is precious. AbbathorÊs greed manifests itself frequently in his actions. Should he set eyes upon any magical item, or on valuable treasure, he will attempt to steal it outright or slay the owner and then take it.

Abbathor wears leather armor and furs made from the skins of beings and creatures who have opposed him in the past. He uses a dagger with a diamond blade and jewels set into the hilt. The dagger can detect the presence of precious metals. No one but Abbathor can grasp this weapon safely. Abbathor also owns a shield that blinds any opponent. Abbathor maintains an uneasy truce with Vergadain, with whom he sometimes roams the Prime Material Plane in search of more treasure. If frustrated when attempting to steal an item, Abbathor will try to destroy the item, so as not to be tortured by the memory of his failure.

AbbathorÊs priesthood secretly opposes that of Dumathoin and, to a lesser extent, Berronar. Clerics must amass gems and sacrifice these (with blood) to Abbathor at least once a year. A small number of his clergy are duergar cleric/thieves, but they very rarely steal from other dwarf unless certain they cannot be observed. Clerical level titles are as follows: Gold Seeker, Coveter of Copper, Seeker of Silver, Luster of Electrum, Hoarder of Gold, Plunderer of Platinum, Miser of Mithral, Hand of Greed, and Master of Greed.
