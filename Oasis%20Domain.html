1st Level: Additional Proficiencies

At 1st level, you gain proficiency with heavy armor and the Survival skill.

1st Level: Keeper of Springs

At 1st level, you can use your action to touch a creature and provide it with 1 hit point. Additionally, the creature is refreshed as if it had met its water needs for the day. You can use this feature a number of times equal to 1 +your Wisdom modifier (minimum of 1). When you finish a long rest you regain all expended uses. A single creature can benefit from this ability only once per long rest.

2nd Level: Channel Divinity: Purifying Spring

At 2nd level, you can use your Channel Divinity to create a spring of holy water that heals all who imbibe. As an action, you present your holy symbol and choose an empty square within 60 feet. A spring with a healing pool equal to 5 x your cleric level gushes from the ground. Creatures adjacent to the spring can spend a bonus action to draw healing from the pool, up to one-half of their maximum hit points. Additionally, creatures may use 5 points of healing from the pool to cure themselves of a disease or poison or a blinded, deafened, or poisoned condition currently affecting them.

6th Level: Oasis Stone

At 6th level, you can spend 8 hours crafting an oasis stone, which holds a reservoir of magical power. As long as you hold the stone. you can infuse your spells with extra power. Whenever you cast an abjuration, evocation, or transmutation spell of 1st level or higher on a single friendly creature, you can cure the creature hit points equal to 2 + the spell's level.

Additionally, you can use an action to consume the stone's power and regain hit points equal to your cleric level. The stone's power can be restored after a long rest.

8th Level: Divine Strike

At 8th level, once on each of your turns you can infuse your weapon strikes with divine energy, causing the attack to deal an extra 1d8 cold damage. When you reach 14th level, the extra damage increases to 2d8.

17th Level: Aqueous Infusion

At 17th level, you can use your action to unleash the power of your oasis stone even more fully, harnessing life in a burst of energy. Choose one of the following effects. Your oasis stone is destroyed and cannot be recreated until after you finish a long rest.

  • Fountain of Life: You cast raise dead on a creature you touch with the oasis stone without expending a spell slot or needing to have the spell prepared.
  • Fountain of Health: You remove all curses, diseases, and poisons affecting a creature you touch with the stone, which also restores all of its hit points.

Fluff

Life in the desert is difficult, and caravans that cross them rely on the oases for their journey. In many places they become home to small shrines, and benevolent desert deities often empower their servants to maintain and create these outposts on their own. Divinities with this domain might include the goddesses Al-Uzza or Allat, the Titan Tethys, or the Egyptian Ash or Tefnut. Clerics who worship these gods can become a spring of refreshment to their allies.
