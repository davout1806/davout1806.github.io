1st Level: Armed for Spiritual Warfare

you either gain proficiency in martial weapons and heavy armor.

1st Level: Crusader’s Devotion

you are always under the effects of a Protection From Evil And Good.

2nd Level: Channel Divinity: Crusader’s Command

you can use your Channel Divinity to Turn celestials and fiends in the same manner as Turn Undead. However, you may choose to charm celestial targets instead of turning them. Celestial targets successfully turned are charmed for 1 minute or until the target takes any damage. However, a celestial that would be destroyed by your turn attempt, can instead be charmed for 1 minute with no risk of the charm being broken by taking damage.

You have a telepathic link to charmed targets that you use to issue commands, which the targets obey to the best of their ability. If the target completes an order and does not receive further direction from you, it will defend and preserve itself to the best of its ability.

Additionally, you can use your action to take control of the target until the end of your next turn, allowing you to dictate the actions of the creature using actions available to that creature. As a reaction, you can cause the target to use a reaction.

6th Level: Channel Divinity: Conjure Celestial

you can use your Channel Divinity to conjure a minor celestial. As an action, you summon a celestial that appears in an unoccupied space that you can see within 30 feet. You can summon one celestial of challenge rating 1 or lower. The celestial disappears when they drop to 0 hit points or when 1 minute has passed.

The celestial is friendly to you and your companions for the duration. Roll Initiative for the celestial, which has its own turns. It obeys any verbal command that you issue it (no action required by you), as long as it doesn’t violate its alignment. If you don’t issue any commands to the celestial, it defends itself from hostile creatures but otherwise take no actions.

The DM has the celestial’s stats.

When you use this feature at higher levels, you choose to summon greater celestials. At each third level after 6th (9, 12, 15 and 18), the maximum challenge rating of the celestial you can summon increases by 1 to a maximum of CR 5 at 18th level.

8th Level: Potent Spellcasting

you may add your Wisdom bonus to the damage you deal with cleric cantrips.

17th Level: Angelic Herald

you can assume the form of an angelic herald. Using your action, you undergo a transformation. For 1 minute, you gain the following benefits:

  • Wings sprout from your back and grant you a Fly speed equal to your current walking speed.
  • You emanate an aura of radiance that provides bright light in a 30-foot radius and dim light shines 30 feet beyond that. Whenever an enemy creature starts its turn in the bright light, the creature takes 1d10 radiant damage. In addition, for the duration, you have advantage on saving throws against spells cast by fiends or undead.

Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finished a long rest.

Fluff

Good deities simply encompass all deities that fall within the good alignment spectrum. The few clerics of these deities that follow the path of good are those devout crusaders who champion the pure cause of good versus evil rather than focusing on some direct aspect of their deity’s portfolio. They are often referred to as Crusaders of the Light.
