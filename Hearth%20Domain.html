1st Level: Bonus Proficiencies

You gain proficiency with heavy armor, mason’s tools and cook’s utensils.

2nd Level: Channel Divinity: Stoke the Embers

You can use your Channel Divinity to ignite a bonfire of divine warmth.

As an action, choose an unoccupied space within 30 feet of you. A bright bonfire appears before you and radiates an aura of warmth that imbues you and your allies with properties of your choice. The bonfire can be up to 5 feet tall and 5 feet wide. In addition to getting doused by an appropriate amount of water, sand or dirt, the bonfire can be extinguished by using any methods that a creature would normally use to extinguish a bonfire.

The bonfire lasts for 1 minute or until you use this feature again.

The bonfire is closely tied to your vitality. If you move more than 40 feet away from the bonfire, it fades and turns to ash. The bonfire also fades if you fall unconscious.

You can create a permanent bonfire by using this feature in the same place each day for three months. A permanent bonfire does not disappear upon using this Channel Divinity feature again, nor does it disappear upon falling unconscious or when moving further than 40 feet away from it.

Each type of bonfire has different properties. Upon using this feature, choose a type of bonfire from this list of options:

Flame of Endurance:

You and friendly creatures within 30 feet of the bonfire gains a number of temporary hit points equal to 1d8 + your Wisdom modifier. The number of temporary hit points increases by 1d8 for every turn a creature remains within 30 feet of the bonfire.

Blaze of Bravery:

You and friendly creatures within 30 feet of the bonfire has advantage on attack rolls, Wisdom saving throws and Charisma saving throws, as well as resistance to psychic damage and necrotic damage. They also become immune to being frightened.

Pyre of Prevention:

Enemies within 30 feet of the bonfire are considered to be in difficult terrain, in addition to having disadvantage on opportunity attacks made against you and your allies. Enemies who begin their turn within 30 feet of the bonfire takes radiant damage equal to 2d8 + your Wisdom modifier.

6th Level: Peaceful Interlude

You gain the ability to help your allies focus in the middle of a stressful adventure.

Upon initiating a short rest, up to three other creatures of your choice recover expended spell slots. The spell slots can have a combined level that is equal to or less than half your Cleric level (rounded up), and none of the slots can be of 4th level or higher.

Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

8th Level:Divine Strike

You gain the ability to infuse your weapon strikes with divine energy. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal an extra 1d8 radiant damage to the target. When you reach 14th level, the extra damage increases to 2d8.

17th Level: Safe Haven

The hearths you provide glows stronger than ever before. Whenever you use your Channel Divinity, a creature of your choice gains resistance to bludgeoning, piercing and slashing damage until the end of your next turn.

In addition, your deity bolsters you with the resolve to aid your allies before taking care of yourself, even in the darkest of times. You gain the ability to use your Peaceful Interlude feature once every short rest, and you only need four hours of sleep to gain the effects of a long rest.

Fluff

Deities of the Hearth – such as Hestia, Frigg, Bastet, Hathor, Chauntea and Brigid – seek to provide safety and comfort. They bless both villages and castles alike, warding off wild beasts and protecting people from those who seek to harm them. Deities of the Hearth see family and marriage as the foundations of a healthy society, prompting their worshippers to foster children and providing a home for them to return to. Some clerics of the Hearth travel the world to build sanctuaries: establishing zones of divinity that keeps the common man safe. Other clerics are tasked to fortify throne rooms and fortresses for kingdoms at war, while some take a more active approach in removing evil from where it originates. Wherever the cleric walks, holy flames trail the ground – bringing heat to friend and foe alike.
