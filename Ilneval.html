(IHL-nee-vahl)

When Gruumsh does not have time to command his armies, he turns the job over to his chief lieutenant, Ilneval, the master of command and strategy (next to Gruumsh himself, of course). Ilneval is the symbol of the leader type, the one who plunges into battle with nothing but victory and destruction on his mind. It is hinted that Ilneval covets Gruumsh's position as chief god of the orcs; he has been said to depose one or two other orc gods, relegating them to lesser status, in his climb to power. Ilneval is more of a "captain's god" than a god of the common orc soldier; Gruumsh is preferred by chieftains and orc kings, and Bahgtru by common warriors. Though Gruumsh does not trust Ilneval, He-Who-Never-Sleeps has Bahgtru on his side, and this relieves some of his concerns.

Ilneval wears a suit of red iron chain mail and wields a sword that slays all non-orcs it strikes. He cannot be touched by missile weapons because of his armor's magical powers, and he is immune to non-magical weapons as well. He appears to be a very war-wise and confident being; his face and arms are heavily scarred from the many battles he has fought, but the scars only increase his appeal to his orc followers.

The half-orc clerics of Ilneval use broadswords as weapons and are multi-classed cleric/fighters. Clergy of all sorts (orc and half-orc) wear red metallic armor and are expected to be good military leaders as well as good clerics. They also strive to ensure that Ilneval's cult is dominant over all others within their clans. Initiates of Ilneval (level 0) are known as the Bloody Blades. Acolytes of Ilneval (levels 1>2) are known as the Blood Mailed, and Ilneval's priests (level 3+) are known as the Hell Swords.
